Volatility Stars

Institutional hedging detection. Volatility Stars identifies correlated volatility events by tracking simultaneous spikes in VIX and VXN. When both indices spike together, it's a regime-shift signal: broad risk is being repriced, not just rotated.

What the "Star" is telling you

  • Hedging is active: institutions are adjusting exposure; liquidity conditions and behavior shift.
  • Decision-point context: the signal is most useful at meaningful locations (key levels, extremes, prior day arrays).
  • Not a standalone entry: it's a high-impact filter and context tool (when to press vs stand down).

How to interpret it (context first)

  • Overextended down + star: potential capitulation/hedging near lows → reversal context if price begins to stabilize.
  • At all-time highs + star: caution signal — hedging into highs; risk of sharp repricing increases.
  • Extreme whipsaw + star: prioritize risk control and patience; wait for volatility to compress before executing.
Execution timing: the spike is the alert. Often the better execution comes after the volatility event, as price transitions from chaos into structured compression/expansion.

Expansion Zones

Statistical range projections. Expansion Zones maps expected intraday delivery from the daily open using net change and standard deviation. It gives you objective "where" (targets and extremes) so you stop guessing.

Core concept

  • Net change-based: focuses on directional close relative to open (institutional execution relevance), not just high-low range.
  • Probability zones: as price reaches higher deviations, continuation becomes less probable and risk/reward changes.

How traders use it

  • Objective targets: manage winners into zones (partials, runners, exit planning).
  • Chase filter: avoid "late" entries once price is already statistically stretched.
  • Reaction map: identify where price is likely to stall, reject, or accelerate further (context dependent).
Best practice: treat zones as a map, not a signal. Pair them with location + confirmation (VWAP context, orderflow behavior, compression/absorption).

VWAP Engine

The complete institutional VWAP system. VWAP Engine is built around how institutions measure execution and define fair value. It combines multi-timeframe VWAPs, deviation bands, and execution VWAPs to give you a mechanical framework for context and timing.

The three VWAP components

  • Extended VWAPs: "memory" levels from prior timeframe VWAP closes that often behave like support/resistance.
  • Institutional VWAPs: multi-timeframe VWAP with deviation bands to quantify stretch and mean reversion odds.
  • Execution VWAPs: rolling VWAPs used for reclaim/breakdown confirmation and entry timing (structure + flow).

Deviation bands (why they matter)

  • ±1 SD: normal flow / comfort zone
  • ±2 SD: statistically stretched; mean reversion algorithms become more active
  • ±3 SD: extreme; reversal probability increases and risk is adjusted
Workflow: use VWAP Engine for location + regime, then use Orderflow+ to confirm participation and execute with defined invalidation.

Orderflow+

The complete orderflow execution framework. Orderflow+ combines compression detection, volume classification, and absorption identification into one system. It tells you what is happening (expansion, absorption, quiet, rejection) and where the mechanical entry is (compression ranges with defined risk).

Compression Volume Classification Absorption Breakout Grading Rejection Detection Measured Targets

Compression detection

  • Institutions need liquidity. Large positions are built in tight ranges where the market can absorb flow.
  • The box gives you invalidation and measured expansion. Compression is actionable because risk is defined.
  • Two trade models: regular breakout (range resolves with acceptance) or failure/trap model (initial break fails, reversal expands with fuel from trapped participants).

Volume classification

  • High Volume (Blue): expansion bar — participation is driving direction.
  • Absorption (Red): high volume with tight range — institutions absorbing flow (key edge).
  • Low Volume (Yellow): minimal participation — dead zones / low quality conditions.
  • Normal Volume (Gray): baseline flow — healthy trends show mostly gray with periodic blue.
  • Rejection Candle (Lime): wick/close structure showing clean rejection — overrides volume color at key locations.

How traders use it (mechanical)

  • Spot absorption early: repeated red prints at a level = real positioning, not random noise.
  • Confirm acceptance: blue prints during breakout = participation is pushing through the range.
  • Avoid chop: low-quality flow (yellow) is usually not worth forcing trades into.
  • Define risk mechanically: compression box gives you invalidation — no more arbitrary stop placement.
Workflow: Use VWAP Engine for location + regime, then Orderflow+ to confirm participation and execute with defined invalidation. The better the location + compression quality, the cleaner the expansion.

Also in the Discord library: HTF Absorption Detection (auto-marks compression zones after consecutive absorption prints with projected targets) and Candle Character (failed breakouts, valid breakouts, and "Judas" sweep patterns).

Candle Character (Library)

Pattern classification for traps and acceptance. Classifies candles based on how they sweep the prior range and where they close, revealing acceptance vs rejection and trapped participants.

Common patterns

  • Failed Breakout: sweep then close back inside → rejection / trap
  • Valid Breakout: sweep then close outside → acceptance
  • Inside Bar: contraction/indecision
  • "Judas" sweeps: liquidity taken on both sides → high signal when paired with location
This tool is included in the Discord library and pairs naturally with Orderflow+ and Compression models.
